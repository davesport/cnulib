Journal: BMC Musculoskeletal Disorders.

What characteristics are we looking for in a journal?

You can find information about a journal on its website. Here's the website for BMC Musculoskeletal Disorders.

  • Open Access - fully, and without delay in releasing articles. This isn't necessary, and doesn't indicate a journal's quality, but it is nice to know we can access this journal's articles freely online.
  • Peer-reviewed - a journal that practices peer-review adds an extra assessment step to its publication requirements, meaning its articles have been rigorously assessed for their quality.
  • Aim: "The prevention, diagnosis and management of musculoskeletal disorders, as well as related molecular genetics, pathophysiology, and epidemiology." The aim can tell you why an article was published by a specific journal.
  • Scope: "The scope of the journal covers research into rheumatic diseases where the primary focus relates specifically to a component(s) of the musculoskeletal system." This tells you what other articles you can expect from a journal.
